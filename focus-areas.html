Our Frameworks

The Integrated Community Response Model

Three interlocking pillars that create a self-sustaining cycle of health, wealth, and ecological restoration.

Pillar 1: Community Health Network
PILLAR 1

Health & One Health Architecture

TTI's "One Health" approach ensures the community is healthy enough to work the land, and the land is productive enough to fund the clinic. A self-replenishing cycle of health and wealth.

  • Community health outreach integrated with agricultural productivity
  • Agricultural surpluses fund youth-friendly primary healthcare services
  • Bridging the gap between environmental health and human wellbeing
Pillar 2: Technical Incubator Lab
PILLAR 2

Economic Empowerment

Animal feed accounts for nearly 70% of poultry and fish production costs in Uganda. By Month 4, our Black Soldier Fly (BSF) colony creates a permanent Feed Bank — reducing external feed dependency by 50%.

  • 50% reduction in external feed dependency protects profit margins
  • 10-15 student cohorts per semester trained as technical leads
  • Hub-certified graduates launch satellite Spoke franchise models

The Knowledge & Commercial Loop

STEM & Vocational Training

Training fees from youth pursuing careers in the Green Economy, turning the Hub into a functional workspace laboratory.

Eco-Tourism & Research

Technical tours for regional NGOs, learning institutions, and international researchers studying the highly effective BSF model.

The Industrial Value-Addition Loop

In Year 3, TTI moves into processing banana pseudo-stems into Eco-Flow Sanitary Towels — shifting from farming to industrial manufacturing with higher margins.
