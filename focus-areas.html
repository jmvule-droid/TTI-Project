Focus Areas

Climate Action, Economic Empowerment, Health.

We don't run three separate programmes. Every waste collection, every spoke launch, every batch of feed and fertiliser is built to move all three at once.

The Loop

From bin to soil, in five moves.

The Black Soldier Fly larva is the engine: it eats what would otherwise rot and leak value out of the system, and turns it into two things farmers already need.

Collection

Spokes gather organic waste from markets, breweries, and households.

→

BSF Rearing

Larvae consume the waste at the hub, converting it in days, not months.

→

Protein Feed

Harvested larvae are dried and milled into feed for poultry, fish, and livestock.

→

Frass Fertiliser

Larvae castings become an organic fertiliser that rebuilds depleted soil.

→

Community Wealth

Spoke leads sell both outputs locally, keeping income inside the community.

The Three Pillars

One intervention, three outcomes.

A single truckload of collected waste moves all three pillars at once. Here's how each one works.

Climate Action

Diverting organic waste from open dumping and burning cuts methane and black-carbon emissions at the source, while frass fertiliser rebuilds soil carbon that synthetic inputs strip out.

Waste diverted · Emissions avoided · Soil restored

Economic Empowerment

Women and youth are trained as technical leads and take ownership of a spoke — not employed by it. Locally produced feed and fertiliser also cut input costs for surrounding farmers.

Spoke ownership · Skilled income · Lower input costs

Health

Removing organic waste from streets and drains cuts breeding grounds for disease vectors, while protein-rich feed improves livestock health — and household nutrition and income downstream.

Vector control · Livestock health · Nutrition security
A single truckload of collected waste → less methane, one trained technical lead, one healthier neighbourhood.
Climate Action

Every tonne diverted is a tonne that doesn't rot in the open.

Organic waste left in open dumps or burned generates methane and black carbon — both far more potent near-term warming agents than CO₂. Routing that waste through BSF rearing intercepts it before it degrades that way.

What comes out the other end — frass — returns organic matter and nutrients to soils that have been mined by years of intensive farming, rebuilding carbon content instead of depleting it further.

Economic Empowerment

Ownership, not employment, is the unit of change.

A job pays until it doesn't. A spoke is an asset a technical lead owns — collection routes, rearing stock, a customer base for feed and fertiliser — and can grow, pass on, or expand into a second spoke.

Because spokes buy less imported feed and fertiliser as they scale, the same intervention also lowers input costs for every smallholder farmer they sell to.

Health

Cleaner streets, better-fed animals, better-fed households.

Standing organic waste is a breeding ground for flies and other disease vectors. Regular spoke collection removes that risk at the neighbourhood level, not just at the hub.

Downstream, protein-dense BSF feed improves the health of the poultry, fish, and livestock that consume it — which in turn improves the nutrition and income of the households that raise them.
