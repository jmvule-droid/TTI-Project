Why We're Different

Two horizons, one hub.

Every spoke we launch is built to pay off immediately for the person running it, and compound for years across the community around it.

Short Term

Job Seekers Become Technical Entrepreneurs

We break the structural barriers of poverty by training youth and women as technical leads who own and run their own spoke — not wait to be hired by someone else's.

Long Term

Healthier Soil, Less Imported Input

Frass fertiliser rebuilds depleted soil and cuts reliance on expensive imported synthetic inputs — plugging the leakage of our communities' wealth and potential.

The Loop

From bin to soil, in five moves.

The Black Soldier Fly larva is the engine: it eats what would otherwise rot and leak value out of the system, and turns it into two things farmers already need.

Collection

Spokes gather organic waste from markets, breweries, and households.

→

BSF Rearing

Larvae consume the waste at the hub, converting it in days, not months.

→

Protein Feed

Harvested larvae are dried and milled into feed for poultry, fish, and livestock.

→

Frass Fertiliser

Larvae castings become an organic fertiliser that rebuilds depleted soil.

→

Community Wealth

Spoke leads sell both outputs locally, keeping income inside the community.

In Every Intervention

Three pillars, one hub.

We don't run separate climate, livelihood, and health programmes. Every spoke we launch is built to move all three at once.

Climate Action

Diverting organic waste from open dumping and burning cuts emissions at the source, while frass fertiliser rebuilds soil carbon.

Waste diverted · Emissions avoided · Soil restored

Economic Empowerment

Women and youth are trained as technical leads who own a spoke — not employed by it — while local feed and fertiliser cut input costs nearby.

Spoke ownership · Skilled income · Lower input costs

Health

Removing organic waste from streets and drains cuts disease vector breeding grounds, while protein feed improves livestock and household nutrition.

Vector control · Livestock health · Nutrition security
