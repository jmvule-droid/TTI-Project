About Us

An incubator built around a hub.

Together for Transformation Initiatives exists to stop resource leakage and build community resilience — one trained technical lead, one spoke at a time.

Our Story

Not just a farm. An incubator.

Every input we intercept — market waste, brewery byproduct, abattoir offcuts — is resource leaking out of the local economy. TTI's model plugs that leak by training the people closest to it to own the fix, using Black Soldier Fly larvae to convert organic waste into high-protein animal feed and fertiliser.

The Hub

A central facility where Black Soldier Fly larvae are bred, and organic waste streams are received, sorted, and converted at scale. The hub houses the technical know-how, quality control, and the training floor.

From here, larvae biomass becomes two products: a high-protein feed ingredient for poultry, fish, and livestock farmers, and a nutrient-dense organic fertiliser (frass) for smallholder soils.

The Spokes

Each spoke is a small, community-based operation — a waste collection point, a rearing unit, a local distribution channel — run independently by a woman or young person who trained at the hub and now owns their segment of the value chain.

Spokes keep waste, labour, and income circulating inside the community instead of leaking to landfill, to imported feed, or to synthetic fertiliser.

Resource Leakage

What we're stopping

Organic waste dumped or burned. Feed and fertiliser imported at rising cost. Youth and women's labour under-used or unpaid.

Community Resilience

What we're building

Local ownership of a circular supply chain — collection, conversion, and sale of feed and fertiliser stay within reach of the community.

Training Pipeline

How a woman or young person becomes a technical lead.

The incubator exists to move people from "waste worker" to "enterprise owner" — with real technical grounding at every stage.

01

Recruit

Women and youth from waste-affected communities are identified and enrolled, with priority for those already informally collecting or trading waste.

02

Train

Hands-on instruction in BSF rearing, waste sorting and safety, feed and frass processing, and basic enterprise bookkeeping.

03

Launch a Spoke

Graduates receive starter stock, equipment, and a defined catchment area to begin collection, rearing, or distribution.

04

Mentor Network

Spoke leads stay connected to the hub for quality control, bulk sales support, and coaching new recruits behind them.

Why Technical Leads, Not Employees

Ownership is what makes resilience stick. A technical lead who owns their spoke has reason to keep collecting, keep training others, and keep the loop closed long after any single programme cycle ends.
